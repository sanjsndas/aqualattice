Comprehensive Cookie Policy

At Aqualattice Irrigation, we are committed to transparency about how we use cookies and similar technologies on our website. This policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and your choices regarding their use.

1. What Are Cookies?

Definition: Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the website owners.

Functionality: Cookies perform various functions, such as remembering your preferences, enabling navigation between pages efficiently, and improving the user experience. They can also help ensure that the advertisements you see online are more relevant to you and your interests.

Types of Cookies: There are several types of cookies, including session cookies (temporary cookies that remain until you close your browser) and persistent cookies (cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them).

First-party vs Third-party: First-party cookies are set by the website you're visiting, while third-party cookies are set by a different domain. Third-party cookies are often used for advertising and analytics purposes.

Similar Technologies: We also use similar technologies like web beacons, pixels, and local storage that may store information on your device to provide certain functionality.

2. How We Use Cookies

Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you such as logging in or filling in forms.

Performance Cookies: These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us know which pages are popular and how visitors move around the site.

Functional Cookies: These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages.

Targeting Cookies: These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant ads on other sites.

Social Media Cookies: These cookies are set by social media services we've added to our site to enable you to share our content with your networks. They may track your browser across other sites.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Analytics Services: We use Google Analytics to help us understand how our customers use the site. You can read more about how Google uses your personal information here: https://policies.google.com/privacy.

Advertising Networks: We work with advertising partners who may set cookies to deliver targeted advertisements that may be of interest to you on other websites.

Social Media Platforms: We use social media buttons and/or plugins on this site that allow you to connect with your social network in various ways. These social media sites may set cookies through our site.

Embedded Content: Pages on this site may include embedded content (e.g., videos, maps). Embedded content from other websites behaves in the exact same way as if the visitor has visited the other website.

Payment Processors: When you make payments through our site, payment processors may set cookies to facilitate the transaction and prevent fraud.

5. Cookie Management

Browser Controls: Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. However, if you limit the ability of websites to set cookies, you may worsen your overall user experience.

Opt-Out Tools: For analytics and advertising cookies, you can use tools like the Digital Advertising Alliance's opt-out page or the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page.

Do Not Track: Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" feature that lets you tell websites you don't want to be tracked. We currently do not respond to DNT signals.

Mobile Devices: Your mobile operating system may provide additional options for opting out of interest-based advertising or limiting ad tracking.

Cookie Preferences: You can manage your cookie preferences for our site using the cookie consent tool that appears when you first visit our website.

6. Cookie Duration

Session Cookies: These temporary cookies remain on your device until you close your browser. They are essential for secure areas of the site and for maintaining your session state.

Persistent Cookies: These remain on your device for the period specified in the cookie (from minutes to years). They activate each time you visit the website that created that particular cookie.

First-party Cookies: Our own cookies typically have shorter durations, usually expiring within a year unless they're essential for site functionality.

Third-party Cookies: Cookies set by our partners may have longer durations as determined by those third parties, often ranging from several months to a couple of years.

7. Legal Basis for Cookie Use

Consent: For non-essential cookies, we rely on your consent as the legal basis for processing. This consent is obtained through our cookie banner when you first visit our site.

Legitimate Interest: For essential cookies, we have a legitimate interest in using them as they're necessary for the website to function properly and provide the services you request.

Contractual Necessity: Some cookies are necessary for us to perform our contractual obligations to you, such as maintaining your shopping cart or processing payments.

Legal Compliance: Certain cookies may be necessary for us to comply with legal obligations, such as those related to security and fraud prevention.

8. Data Collected by Cookies

Device Information: Cookies may collect information about the device you're using, including browser type, operating system, IP address, and device identifiers.

Usage Data: They may track how you interact with our website, including pages visited, time spent on pages, and links clicked.

Location Data: Some cookies may collect approximate location information based on your IP address to provide localized content.

Referral Data: Cookies may track how you arrived at our site (e.g., through a search engine, social media, or direct visit).

Form Data: Cookies may remember information you've entered in forms to make future interactions easier.

9. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Essential Features: If you disable essential cookies, parts of the site will not work, including login areas, shopping carts, and checkout processes.

User Experience: Disabling functional cookies may mean the website doesn't remember your preferences, leading to a less personalized experience.

Performance Data: Without analytics cookies, we won't be able to gather data about how users interact with our site, making improvements more difficult.

Advertising: Opting out of targeting cookies means you may still see ads, but they'll be less relevant to your interests.

10. Cookie Policy Updates

Regular Reviews: We regularly review our cookie usage and this policy to ensure it remains accurate and up-to-date with current practices.

Notification of Changes: When we make significant changes to our cookie practices, we'll update this policy and may notify users through our website or other channels.

Version Control: This policy includes a version number and date of last update at the bottom of the page for reference.

Historical Comparisons: Previous versions of this policy will be archived and available upon request for comparison purposes.

11. International Considerations

GDPR Compliance: For users in the European Union, we comply with the General Data Protection Regulation regarding cookie consent and data collection.

CCPA Compliance: For California residents, we honor requests to opt-out of the sale of personal information as defined by the California Consumer Privacy Act.

Other Jurisdictions: We strive to comply with applicable privacy laws in all jurisdictions where our website is accessible.

12. Children's Privacy

Age Restrictions: Our website is not intended for children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children through cookies or other means.

Parental Controls: We encourage parents to monitor their children's online activities and consider using parental control tools to manage cookie settings.

13. Security Measures

Secure Cookies: Where appropriate, we use secure cookies that are only transmitted over encrypted (HTTPS) connections.

HttpOnly Flags: Certain cookies are marked as HttpOnly, meaning they can't be accessed by client-side scripts for added security.

SameSite Attributes: We implement SameSite cookie attributes to protect against certain types of cross-site request forgery attacks.
