Expert Irrigation Tips

Practical advice for getting the most from your irrigation system.

Optimizing Water Pressure

Proper water pressure is crucial for efficient irrigation. Too high and you risk damaging emitters; too low and coverage will be uneven. For Aqualattice systems, we recommend maintaining pressure between 20-30 psi. Install a pressure regulator if your supply exceeds this range.

Signs of pressure problems include misting (too high) or uneven water distribution (too low). Check pressure at different times of day, as municipal supplies often fluctuate.

Zone Planning Strategies

Group plants with similar water needs together in the same irrigation zone. Consider these factors when zoning:

  • Plant type (succulents vs. leafy vegetables)
  • Sun exposure (full sun areas dry faster)
  • Soil type (sandy soils drain faster than clay)
  • Slope (water runs downhill)

Most urban gardens benefit from at least 3-4 zones. Our designers can help create an optimal zone plan for your space.

Preparing for Winter

In cold climates, proper winterization prevents freeze damage:

  1. Turn off water supply and drain all pipes
  2. Use compressed air to blow out remaining water (or our winterization service)
  3. Insulate above-ground components
  4. Remove and store timers/controllers indoors
  5. Mark system locations for easy spring startup

In mild climates, simply reduce watering frequency but maintain system operation to prevent seal drying.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Dry spots - Check for clogged emitters or low pressure in that zone. May need additional emitters.

Problem: Leaks - Tighten connections or replace damaged components. Most leaks occur at joints.

Problem: System won't turn on - Check power source, controller programming, and valve operation.

Problem: High water bill - Look for leaks, excessive runtime, or inefficient scheduling (avoid midday watering).
